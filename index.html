DM1 Research

Myotonic dystrophy type 1 (DM1) is a genetic disorder that affects many parts of the body. It is the most prevalent form of myotonic dystrophies, affecting 1 in 8,000 people worldwide. There are regional differences in prevalence, for example in New York State the prevalence is 1 in 2,000. At the molecular level, the cause of DM1 is the transcription of a CTG microsatellite repeat that creates toxic-CUG RNA which have a gain-of-function, trapping important RNA-binding proteins involved in regulating the formation of messenger RNA. In Andy Berglund's Lab, we study DM1 using biological, biochemical, chemical, and bioinformatic techniques. My projects revolve around therapeutic strategies designed to interfere with toxic-CUG RNA from trapping RNA-binding proteins.

Quercetin selectively reduces expanded repeat RNA levels in models of myotonic dystrophy.
Subodh K. Mishra, Sawyer M. Hicks, Jesus A. Frias, Sweta Vangaveti, Masayuki Nakamori, John D. Cleary, Kaalak Reddy, J. Andrew Berglund. BioRxiv 2023.

In this project, I was the bioinformatic lead on processing and analyzing RNA-seq data. I used differential gene expression and alternative splicing tools to confirm beneficial effects of enzymatically modified isoquercetin to alternative splicing and gene expression in a DM1 mouse model.

More Research

Notch signaling determines cell-fate specification of the two main types of vomeronasal neurons of rodents.
Raghu Ram Katreddi, Ed Zandro M. Taroc, Sawyer M. Hicks, Jennifer M. Lin, Shuting Liu, Mengqing Xiang, and Paolo E. Forni. Development 2022.

During my rotation in Dr. Paolo Forni's lab I contributed to this project by processing and analyzing single-cell RNA-seq data, helping to identify important genes and transcription factors which served as markers for vomeronasal neuron cell-fate specification.

Loss of function of male-specific lethal 3 (Msl3) does not affect spermatogenesis in rodents
T. A. Mitchell, J. M. Lin, S. M. Hicks, J. R. James, P. Rangan, P. E. Forni. Developmental Dynamics 2023.

In this work, I searched for, collated, and mined Drop-Seq and 10X Genomics single-cell RNA-seq datasets. Using the R packages Seurat and Monocle3, I mapped expression patterns of Msl3 expression in wild-type mice testes during spermatogenesis and provided leads on related genes involved in this biological process.
